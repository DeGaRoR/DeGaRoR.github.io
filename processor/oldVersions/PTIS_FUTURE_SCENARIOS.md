
# PTIS_FUTURE_SCENARIOS
Long‑Term Scenario Catalogue for *Process This In Space* (PTIS)

Status: Strategic design document  
Purpose: Maintain a curated list of scenario arcs that can be developed once the core Planet X campaign is complete.  
Scope: High‑level narrative and engineering themes, not mission implementation details.

Principle: New scenarios should primarily reuse the **existing thermodynamics engine** (gas systems, reactions, pressure networks, power grid) with minimal new mechanics.

---

# 1. Core Campaign (Reference Backbone)

The Planet X campaign is the canonical introduction to PTIS.

Mission arc:

1. Survival
2. Water recovery
3. Oxygen production
4. Energy stabilization
5. Sustained life support
6. Cold chain management
7. Energy scaling
8. Fertilizer and agriculture
9. Cryogenic emergency management
10. Biosphere closure

Terraforming is considered **post‑campaign endgame**.

All additional scenarios assume this campaign exists first.

---

# 2. Scenario Design Principles

Future scenarios should:

• emphasize **thermodynamic reasoning**
• use **gas‑phase chemistry and pressure systems**
• create **clear survival or engineering stakes**
• reuse the core engine whenever possible
• avoid mechanics outside the engine’s strengths (electrochemistry, mining, complex liquids)

Preferred scenario style:

> “Build and stabilize a critical thermodynamic system under constraints.”

---

# 3. Scenario Categories

Future content is organized into five families.

1. Real Space Engineering
2. Solar System Expansion
3. Harsh Environment Earth
4. Environmental Engineering
5. Post‑Apocalyptic Earth

Each category contains three flagship missions.

---

# 4. Real Space Engineering

Inspired by real NASA / ESA research.

These missions are credible engineering problems adapted into gameplay.

### ISS Life Support

Maintain a closed life‑support loop on a space station.

Engineering challenges:

• oxygen regeneration  
• CO₂ scrubbing  
• humidity management  
• heat rejection  
• limited power availability  

Goal:

Sustain crew survival indefinitely.

---

### Mars ISRU Plant

Produce oxygen and fuel using Martian atmosphere.

Engineering systems:

• CO₂ capture
• Sabatier reactor
• electrolysis
• cryogenic storage

Goal:

Produce methane and oxygen for a return launch.

---

### Mars Base Expansion

Scale a small survival outpost into a stable habitat.

Challenges:

• atmospheric processing
• power scaling
• air recycling
• thermal stability

Goal:

Support a growing population.

---

# 5. Solar System Expansion

More speculative but grounded missions exploring industrialization of the solar system.

### Titan Fuel Depot

Produce methane and oxygen from local resources to fuel spacecraft.

Systems:

• cryogenic storage
• atmospheric processing
• power from nuclear or wind sources

Goal:

Create a refueling hub for interplanetary travel.

---

### Venus Cloud City

Maintain a floating habitat in the Venusian atmosphere.

Key systems:

• atmospheric separation
• buoyancy management
• extreme corrosion handling

Goal:

Maintain a stable floating colony.

---

### Orbital Fuel Depot

Operate a microgravity cryogenic fuel storage platform.

Challenges:

• boil‑off management
• power balance
• incoming spacecraft docking demands

Goal:

Supply spacecraft traveling through the solar system.

---

# 6. Harsh Environment Earth

Realistic extreme environments on Earth.

### Arctic Research Station

Maintain a research base through polar winter.

Challenges:

• energy scarcity
• oxygen management
• heat loss
• fuel storage

Goal:

Survive until the next supply window.

---

### Volcanic Energy Facility

Exploit geothermal vents to power industrial systems.

Engineering challenges:

• high‑temperature gas streams
• corrosion
• unstable flow rates

Goal:

Stabilize a volatile geothermal plant.

---

### Remote Off‑Grid Settlement

Design a fully autonomous settlement.

Systems:

• renewable energy
• food production
• atmospheric management

Goal:

Achieve complete self‑sufficiency.

---

# 7. Environmental Engineering

Modern planetary‑scale challenges adapted into engineering gameplay.

### Offshore Hydrogen Platform

Produce hydrogen using offshore wind energy.

Challenges:

• intermittent power
• gas storage
• compression
• transport

Goal:

Maintain continuous hydrogen production.

---

### Methane Capture Network

Prevent methane emissions from industrial sources.

Engineering systems:

• gas capture
• compression
• processing

Goal:

Minimize atmospheric methane release.

---

### Carbon Capture Facility

Capture CO₂ from air using large‑scale industrial processes.

Challenges:

• enormous air throughput
• energy consumption
• compression logistics

Goal:

Stabilize a large atmospheric processing facility.

---

# 8. Post‑Apocalyptic Earth

Survival scenarios after global collapse.

These emphasize resilience rather than efficiency.

### Bunker Life Support

Maintain an underground shelter.

Challenges:

• air recycling
• food generation
• water recovery
• limited energy

Goal:

Sustain human life indefinitely.

---

### Contaminated Surface Dome

Create a habitable dome in a toxic environment.

Systems:

• atmosphere purification
• pressure stability
• thermal management

Goal:

Allow safe human activity outside bunkers.

---

### Desert Survival Colony

Build a sustainable settlement in an extreme desert.

Challenges:

• water scarcity
• temperature extremes
• solar power variability

Goal:

Create a permanent colony.

---

# 9. Scenario Selection Strategy

Not all scenarios should be implemented immediately.

Priority order:

1. Real Space Engineering
2. Harsh Environment Earth
3. Solar System Expansion
4. Environmental Engineering
5. Post‑Apocalyptic Earth

Selection should consider:

• gameplay clarity  
• engine compatibility  
• visual storytelling potential  

---

# 10. Long‑Term Vision

PTIS evolves from:

```
Survival Engineering Game
        ↓
Planetary Engineering Sandbox
```

Players begin by surviving in hostile environments and eventually scale systems to planetary engineering challenges.
